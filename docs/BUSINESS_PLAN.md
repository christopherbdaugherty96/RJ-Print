# RJ Print Business Plan

Status: planning baseline

## Business identity

RJ Print is a 3D print shop offering ready-made, made-to-order, and custom 3D printed products.

The business should be presented as practical, creative, and custom-capable without overpromising automation, speed, or production capacity.

## Positioning statement

RJ Print creates ready-made and custom 3D printed products for gifts, home use, decor, accessories, practical items, and personalized ideas.

## Core offer

RJ Print should be organized around four clear business lanes:

1. Ready-Made Prints
   - Products already printed and available for sale.
   - Best for immediate pickup, local sales, markets, photos, and quick purchases.

2. Made-to-Order Prints
   - Standard products the client already knows how to print.
   - Customer may select color, size, finish, or variant if available.

3. Custom Print Requests
   - Quote-based work from customer ideas, photos, measurements, broken parts, gifts, or unique needs.
   - Should not be treated as instant checkout until scope is reviewed.

4. Business / Bulk Orders
   - Larger or repeated work for businesses, events, gifts, displays, signs, branded items, or local organizations.

## Immediate business priorities

1. List current products.
2. Separate ready-made, made-to-order, and custom-only products.
3. Calculate real product costs and margins.
4. Identify best sellers and high-margin products.
5. Create a custom quote process.
6. Collect product photos and customer examples.
7. Build a website around clear customer actions.

## Product decision rules

### Keep

A product should stay active if it has good margin, clear demand, manageable print time, and low failure risk.

### Improve

A product should be improved if people like it but print time, pricing, photos, or material cost make it weak.

### Custom only

A product should be quote-only if it is too variable, slow, fragile, or dependent on customer-specific dimensions.

### Stop or pause

A product should be paused if it has poor margin, too many failed prints, unclear customer interest, or high production friction.

## Revenue paths

- Individual product sales
- Custom print quotes
- Local pickup orders
- Shipped orders
- Bulk orders
- Business/event orders
- Seasonal or gift-focused drops

## Trust signals needed

- Real product photos
- Clear materials and finish expectations
- Production time estimates
- Pickup/shipping explanation
- Custom request process
- FAQ
- Examples of completed work
- Customer reviews only after real permission

## Do not overstate

Do not claim:

- instant production for all orders
- guaranteed same-day turnaround
- unlimited custom work
- fully automated printer fulfillment
- perfect fit for replacement parts without review
- business capacity that has not been proven

## First 30-day goal

Create a clean website and business foundation that lets customers understand what RJ Print sells, request custom work, and buy or inquire with confidence.

## First proof goal

Complete one website-driven inquiry or sale, then document what happened, what product/category performed best, and what should be improved.
