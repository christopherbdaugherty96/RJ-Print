# RJ Print Product Photo And Listing Standard

Status: active launch-quality standard

## Purpose

Define the minimum requirements a product must meet before being promoted on the website, Shopify store, social media, or marketing materials.

## Core rule

A product should not be publicly promoted until customers can clearly understand:

- what it is
- what it looks like
- what material it uses
- how it is obtained
- how long it takes to receive
- whether it is ready-made, made-to-order, custom-only, or bulk

## Minimum photo requirements

Each launch product should have:

- At least 2 clear photos
- At least 1 primary product image
- At least 1 alternate angle image

Recommended:

- Scale reference photo
- In-use photo
- Color variation photo if applicable

## Photo quality requirements

Photos should:

- Be in focus
- Have adequate lighting
- Show the actual product
- Represent color honestly
- Show visible details
- Avoid distracting backgrounds

Avoid:

- Blurry photos
- Heavy filters
- Misleading edits
- Photos that hide defects or limitations

## Product listing requirements

Every public listing should include:

- Product name
- Product lane
- Category
- Price or quote-required label
- Description
- Material information if known
- Production or processing note
- Pickup/shipping note
- Customization information if applicable
- Product photo(s)

## Ready-made products

Should include:

- Current inventory status if tracked
- Pickup/shipping expectation
- Clear photos of the actual item

## Made-to-order products

Should include:

- Estimated production time
- Available options
- Color/material limitations if applicable

## Custom-only products

Should include:

- Quote required label
- Custom request explanation
- No fixed pricing unless approved

## High-risk products

Require review before listing:

- Lamps or electrical products
- Food-contact products
- Children's products
- Medical-related products
- Automotive products
- Safety-critical products
- Load-bearing products

## Real product preference

Real product photos are preferred over mockups.

If mockups are used:

- They should be clearly representative
- They should not imply features that do not exist
- Real product photography should replace them when practical

## Launch gate

A product should not be featured on the homepage or in marketing materials if:

- It lacks photos
- Pricing is unknown
- Production expectations are unclear
- Category assignment is unclear
- Risk review is incomplete when required

## First launch focus

Prioritize:

1. Best current sellers
2. Strong-margin products
3. Products with strong photos
4. Products that are easy to explain
5. Products that can be produced reliably

## Boundary

Do not create product listings that imply capabilities, materials, certifications, safety guarantees, production volume, or fulfillment automation that RJ Print has not verified.
