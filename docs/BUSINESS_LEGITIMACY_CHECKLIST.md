# RJ Print Business Legitimacy Checklist

Status: planning checklist / requires client confirmation

## Purpose

Track the business basics RJ Print should confirm before treating the website or store as an official public business launch.

This document is not legal, tax, or insurance advice. It is a project checklist for collecting decisions and identifying blockers.

## Current truth

RJ Print already has product activity, but the repo has not confirmed whether the business has completed formal setup items such as entity registration, tax registration, banking, insurance, or business records.

Do not claim RJ Print is legally formed, licensed, insured, or tax-ready until the client confirms those items.

## Business identity

- [ ] Confirm exact public business name.
- [ ] Confirm legal business name if different from public name.
- [ ] Confirm owner or owners.
- [ ] Confirm roles and decision-maker for website approval.
- [ ] Confirm public service area.
- [ ] Confirm whether RJ Print is local-only, online, or both.
- [ ] Confirm business email.
- [ ] Confirm business phone if public.
- [ ] Confirm business mailing address or return address plan.
- [ ] Confirm social media handles.
- [ ] Confirm domain preference.

## Name and brand checks

- [ ] Search business name availability.
- [ ] Search domain availability.
- [ ] Search social handle availability.
- [ ] Do basic trademark/name-conflict review before final branding.
- [ ] Confirm whether to use a text logo or designed logo for v1.
- [ ] Confirm brand colors.
- [ ] Confirm tagline if any.

## Entity and registration

- [ ] Decide business structure: sole proprietor, LLC, partnership LLC, or other.
- [ ] Confirm whether RJ Print is already legally registered.
- [ ] If registered, collect entity name and formation date.
- [ ] If not registered, decide whether registration happens before public launch.
- [ ] Confirm local city/township requirements if operating from home or offering pickup.
- [ ] Store business formation documents in a business records folder.

## Tax and sales readiness

- [ ] Confirm EIN status.
- [ ] Confirm state tax registration status.
- [ ] Confirm sales tax license/permit status if selling taxable physical products.
- [ ] Confirm whether Shopify or another platform will collect sales tax.
- [ ] Confirm sales tax filing responsibility.
- [ ] Confirm bookkeeping method.
- [ ] Confirm where receipts and expenses are stored.

## Banking and payments

- [ ] Confirm business bank account status.
- [ ] Confirm payment processor decision.
- [ ] Confirm whether payments are through Shopify, invoice, cash/local, or another method.
- [ ] Confirm deposit rules for custom work.
- [ ] Confirm refund/replacement payment process.
- [ ] Do not mix client business income with unrelated accounts if the business is being operated formally.

## Insurance and risk review

- [ ] Confirm whether RJ Print has general liability insurance.
- [ ] Confirm whether product liability coverage is needed or already active.
- [ ] Confirm whether home-based business coverage is needed.
- [ ] Confirm whether equipment/printer coverage is needed.
- [ ] Confirm whether business/bulk orders create additional coverage needs.
- [ ] Do not claim insured status unless confirmed.

## Records and documentation

- [ ] Create business records folder.
- [ ] Store formation documents.
- [ ] Store EIN/tax documents.
- [ ] Store sales tax/license documents.
- [ ] Store insurance documents.
- [ ] Store supplier receipts.
- [ ] Store product cost records.
- [ ] Store customer quote approvals.
- [ ] Store shipping/refund records.

## Launch rule

RJ Print should not be promoted as a fully official e-commerce business until the client confirms the appropriate legal, tax, payment, policy, and fulfillment basics.

A quote/contact-first website may be launched earlier if it avoids unsupported claims, does not process unverified checkout, and clearly explains custom work and pickup/shipping expectations.
